The Power of Legacy Earlington Legacy Initiative NPC

The **Earlington Legacy Initiative** is being established as a dedicated **Non-Profit Company (NPC)** to formalize and scale the support for Earlington Secondary School. This legal structure ensures **sustained funding, financial integrity, and eligibility for donor tax incentives (PBO status)**.

Our Strategic Pillars

The core mission is driven by four strategic, high-impact programs designed to address fundamental gaps in welfare and opportunity. Click on each card to learn more.

Fund School Needs

Infrastructure & Technology

Fund strategic infrastructure projects, technological upgrades, and educational resources identified and formally validated by the School Governing Body (SGB).

Create Bursary Schemes

Tertiary Education Access

Establish and administer sustainable bursary and scholarship schemes for academically talented and deserving students to pursue tertiary education.

Assist Needy Students

Essential Support & Welfare

Provide essential support and interventions to remove financial barriers to education for vulnerable students, including uniforms, stationary, and academic aid.

Develop Feeding Schemes

Nutrition & Concentration

Fund and oversee programs to ensure students from disadvantaged backgrounds receive adequate nutrition, thereby improving concentration and attendance.

Founding Governance: The Nine-Director Board

The Initiative will be governed by a structured nine-director, skills-based Board. This hierarchy ensures strong financial integrity and strategic mission delivery across all statutory and programmatic functions.

Director Nominations & Support

The founding board is being constituted to ensure a range of skills and independence. We are actively seeking nominations for seven open director positions.

Required Areas of Expertise

We are seeking dedicated individuals with professional expertise in the following areas to fill the seven open Executive, Strategic, and Non-Executive Director roles:

  • Finance & Audit Oversight: Essential for the Treasurer role and the Non-Executive Director focusing on financial compliance and reporting.

  • Legal & Governance: Required for the Secretary role, ensuring adherence to the MOI, NPO Act, and Companies Act.

  • Fundraising & Resource Mobilization: Driving grant applications, corporate partnerships, and public campaigns to secure the Initiative's funding.

  • Programme & Project Evaluation (M&E): Ensuring impact measurement and accountability for the four strategic mission pillars.

  • Educational Liaison / SGB Experience: Providing a direct link and understanding of the school's operational needs and dynamics.
